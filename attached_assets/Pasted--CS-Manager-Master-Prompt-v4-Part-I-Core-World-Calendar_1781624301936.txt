# CS Manager - Master Prompt v4
## Part I - Core World & Calendar Simulation

Create a fully working Python multi-file console game called:

"CS Manager - Living Esports World Simulation"

This game is inspired by Football Manager and the CS2 professional ecosystem.

The game MUST simulate a living esports world that progresses through a structured calendar year.

The game is NOT a collection of standalone matches.

The core gameplay loop revolves around the passage of time, tournament schedules, team development, and ecosystem evolution.

---

# CALENDAR-BASED WORLD SIMULATION

The game world progresses chronologically.

Time advances through:

- Years
- Months
- Weeks
- Match days

The player does NOT manually choose random matches.

Instead, the game automatically schedules competitions according to the esports calendar.

All systems must operate within this calendar.

Example:

January Week 1
→ League matches
→ Training
→ News

January Week 2
→ League matches
→ Tier 2 tournaments
→ Rankings update

February
→ Continue season

March
→ League conclusion
→ Relegation qualifiers
→ Major qualification

---

# GLOBAL STRUCTURE

The world consists of four major regions:

1. Europe
2. Asia
3. Latin America
4. Africa/Oceania

Each region contains:

- a large ecosystem of organizations,
- professional teams,
- academy teams,
- lower-division teams.

Each region should contain approximately 50 organizations.

The exact number may vary.

At minimum, the world must contain 128+ organizations globally.

---

# REGIONAL LEAGUES

Regional leagues are the foundation of the competitive calendar.

Three regional league stages occur every year.

These stages determine:

- Major qualification,
- Regional rankings,
- Relegation,
- Promotion,
- TI points.

Regional leagues are considered Tier 1 competitions.

---

## Regional League Schedule

Winter Regional League:
- January
- February
- March

Spring Regional League:
- April
- May
- June

Summer Regional League:
- August
- September
- October

July is reserved primarily for secondary events.

November contains TI qualification activities.

December contains The International.

---

# REGIONAL LEAGUE FORMAT

Each region hosts its own league.

Each regional league consists of:

16 teams.

These 16 teams are selected based on:

- regional rankings,
- previous league participation,
- promotion/relegation system.

Format:

Round Robin.

Each team plays every other team once.

Total matches per team:

15.

Match format:

Best of 3.

Standings determine:

- Major qualification,
- regional points,
- relegation positions.

---

# PROMOTION / RELEGATION SYSTEM

At the conclusion of each regional league stage:

The bottom three teams are relegated.

These organizations lose their place in the next regional league cycle.

Promotion occurs through qualifiers.

This system ensures that regional leagues evolve over time and do not feature the exact same teams every stage.

---

# OPEN QUALIFIERS

All organizations within a region that are NOT currently participating in the regional league may enter qualifiers.

Example:

Europe:
- 50 organizations total.
- 16 organizations in league.
- 34 organizations eligible for qualifiers.

Qualifier format may be simplified.

Top three organizations earn promotion into the next regional league.

This creates:

- Cinderella stories,
- fresh competition,
- ecosystem mobility.

---

# MAJOR SYSTEM

There are three Majors every year.

Majors are Tier 1 international tournaments.

Schedule:

Major 1:
After Winter Regional League.

Major 2:
After Spring Regional League.

Major 3:
After Summer Regional League.

Major participation is determined by regional performance.

Slot allocation example:

Europe:
6 slots.

Asia:
4 slots.

Latin America:
3 slots.

Africa/Oceania:
3 slots.

Total:
16 teams.

Majors provide:

- prize money,
- Major titles,
- TI qualification points,
- significant HLTV ranking value.

Major performance strongly impacts the global ecosystem.

---

# TIER 2 AND TIER 3 TOURNAMENTS

The world must remain active outside major events.

Secondary tournaments are mandatory.

These tournaments should occur:

- during breaks,
- between major events,
- during periods where organizations are idle.

Tier 2 tournaments include:

- commercial LAN events,
- invitational tournaments,
- online events.

Tier 3 tournaments include:

- developmental competitions,
- academy events,
- regional cups.

Priority participation:

Organizations that:
- failed to qualify for Majors,
- are not currently scheduled,
- belong to lower divisions.

These tournaments provide:

- smaller prize pools,
- limited ranking value,
- additional match experience.

Tier 2 and Tier 3 tournaments MUST NOT override the importance of Majors.

Their influence on TI qualification should remain minor.

---

# THE INTERNATIONAL (TI)

The International is the most prestigious tournament of the year.

It occurs annually in December.

It concludes the competitive calendar.

It offers the largest prize pool.

Minimum prize pool:

At least 2.5 times larger than a Major prize pool.

---

# TI PARTICIPANTS

Total teams:

20.

Qualification occurs through:

1. Seasonal performance.
2. Major performance.
3. TI qualification points.

Most teams receive direct invitations.

Remaining slots are earned through regional qualifiers.

---

# REGIONAL TI QUALIFIERS

Each region hosts a final qualifier.

One team from each region qualifies.

Regions:

- Europe,
- Asia,
- Latin America,
- Africa/Oceania.

Total qualifier slots:

4.

These events represent last-chance opportunities.

---

# TI FORMAT

Stage One:

Swiss System.

All 20 teams begin equally.

Rules:

Three wins:
advance.

Three losses:
eliminated.

Teams always face opponents with identical records.

Examples:

1-1 teams play 1-1 teams.

2-2 teams play 2-2 teams.

This structure continues until qualification or elimination.

---

# TI PLAYOFFS

After Swiss Stage:

The remaining teams enter playoffs.

Playoff structure may use:

Single Elimination.

or

Double Elimination.

The structure should be configurable.

Playoffs determine the world champion.

---

# MONTHLY WORLD PROGRESSION

The world advances naturally.

Every month should include:

- scheduled matches,
- tournament progression,
- rankings updates,
- news generation,
- player development,
- organizational changes.

The player experiences the passage of an esports year rather than selecting isolated matches.

---

# DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The match is NOT the primary object of the game.

The calendar is the primary object.

Matches exist within tournaments.

Tournaments exist within seasons.

Seasons exist within the living esports world.

The goal of the simulation is to make the player feel like they are managing an organization throughout years of esports history rather than simply simulating random games.
